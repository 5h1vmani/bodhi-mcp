# Video Production Craft: Audio, Transitions & Subtitles

> The production decisions that separate amateur from professional video — music tempo matching, cut pacing, and caption styling that measurably lifts engagement.

## TL;DR

- **Match BPM to intent** — 100-130 BPM for energy/promos, <80 BPM for tutorials/trust; fast music actively hurts comprehension of complex content
- **Cut frequency follows content type** — 2-5s for ads, 3.5s for music video chorus, 15s for documentary; average shot length has dropped from 12s (1930s) to 2.5s today
- **Hormozi-style captions are the proven pattern** — Montserrat Bold, yellow/white/green, pop-in animation; captions alone boost retention 40% and view time 12%
- **Subtitle safe zones differ per platform** — YouTube Shorts: center third; Reels: central 70-80% of frame; getting this wrong means text hidden behind UI
- **Music has two separate copyrights** — composition + recording; securing one doesn't cover the other; use royalty-free libraries for commercial video

## Decision Guide

| Scenario                     | Approach                                                                     | Why                                                                                  |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Product launch / promo video | 100-130 BPM music, 2-5s cuts, bold animated captions                         | Fast tempo increases excitement and purchase intent                                  |
| Tutorial / explainer         | <80 BPM ambient, 10-15s shots, clean static captions                         | Fast music hurts comprehension of complex content; longer shots allow processing     |
| Social media (TikTok/Reels)  | 170+ WPM voiceover, whip pans/match cuts, karaoke-style captions             | Platform audiences expect high energy; animated captions essential for muted viewing |
| Sales demo / walkthrough     | 120-150 WPM narration, minimal transitions (cuts only), lower-third captions | Clarity over style; transitions distract from UI being shown                         |
| Brand awareness / emotional  | Slow music with intentional pauses, dissolves/L-cuts, minimal text           | Pauses and slow transitions create emotional weight                                  |
| YouTube long-form            | 150-160 WPM, J/L-cuts for narrative flow, SRT/VTT captions                   | Toggle-able captions for accessibility; J-cuts build intrigue between scenes         |
| CTV / streaming ad           | Broadcast-quality audio, professional dissolves, burnt-in captions           | No viewer controls; captions must be embedded; audio expected                        |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake                                               | Fix                                                                                                         |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Same music energy for all content types               | Match BPM to intent: high energy (100-130) for promos, ambient (<80) for tutorials                          |
| Using platform audio library sounds in commercial ads | Platform sounds (TikTok, Instagram) often licensed personal-only; use Epidemic Sound/Artlist for commercial |
| Subtitles placed at screen edges                      | Stay within platform safe zones: Shorts center third, Reels central 70-80%                                  |
| Static white text on moving backgrounds               | Add black drop shadow or semi-transparent background bar for contrast (4.5:1 WCAG minimum)                  |
| Music same volume throughout                          | Duck music -15 to -20 dB under voiceover; target 2kHz frequency range to clear space for speech             |
| Only securing recording rights, not composition       | Music has two copyrights (composition + recording); both needed for commercial use                          |
| Captions as afterthought                              | Captions are a primary engagement driver (+40% retention, +12% view time); style them intentionally         |
| Same transition style everywhere                      | Match transitions to platform: whip pans/glitch for TikTok, dissolves for YouTube, cuts for demos           |

## Checklist

- [ ] Music BPM matches content intent (check against tempo guide below)
- [ ] Voiceover pacing appropriate for content type (120-170+ WPM range)
- [ ] Music ducked -15 to -20 dB under voiceover
- [ ] Cut frequency matches content type (not too fast for tutorials, not too slow for ads)
- [ ] Captions styled intentionally (font, color, animation — not default auto-gen)
- [ ] Subtitle placement within platform-specific safe zones
- [ ] Caption contrast meets 4.5:1 minimum (WCAG AA)
- [ ] Music commercially licensed (both composition + recording rights)
- [ ] Burnt-in captions for social; SRT/VTT for platform distribution
- [ ] Strategic pauses preserved (not all silence removed)

## Music Tempo Guide

| Content Type            | BPM Range | Why                                                |
| ----------------------- | --------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Product launch / promo  | 100-130   | Increases excitement, arousal, and purchase intent |
| Action / trailer        | 120-140   | Matches rapid visual pacing                        |
| Tutorial / explainer    | 60-80     | Avoids cognitive overload; supports comprehension  |
| Emotional / brand story | 60-90     | Evokes reflection, trust, calmness                 |
| Social media clips      | 100-120   | Matches high-energy platform expectations          |
| Background / ambient    | 70-90     | Non-distracting; supports without competing        |

## Voiceover Pacing

| Content Type              | WPM     | Notes                                     |
| ------------------------- | ------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Narration / documentary   | 120-150 | Clarity priority; room for pauses         |
| E-learning / tutorial     | 140-160 | Balance engagement with comprehension     |
| Audiobook style           | 150-160 | Narrative flow                            |
| Commercial / ad           | 160-180 | Attention-grabbing; compressed timeframe  |
| Social media (short-form) | 170+    | Fast, high-energy; audience expects speed |

**Rule of thumb**: ~130-150 words fills 1 minute at comfortable pace. Technical content needs slower delivery.

## Cut Frequency by Content Type

| Content Type             | Average Shot Length         | Notes                           |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Commercials / ads        | 2-5 seconds                 | Rapid cuts for message delivery |
| Action / trailers        | ~2 seconds                  | Peak sequences; builds energy   |
| Music videos             | 3.5s (chorus), 5-6s (verse) | Tempo-synced cutting            |
| Talking head / interview | 5-10 seconds                | Cut on thought transitions      |
| Tutorial / explainer     | 8-15 seconds                | Allow time for processing       |
| Documentary              | ~15 seconds                 | Balance pacing with depth       |

**Trend**: Average shot length has dropped from 12s (1930s) to 2.5s today. The 3-second rule: viewers fully engage within 3-5 seconds before attention wanes.

## Transition Types

| Transition                       | Use When                                             | Avoid When                                                |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Cut**                          | Default; fast-paced content; screen recordings       | Never bad, but monotonous if overused in creative content |
| **Dissolve**                     | Time passage; emotional shifts; scene changes        | Fast-paced ads; technical demos                           |
| **J-cut** (audio precedes video) | Building intrigue; dialogue transitions; intros      | Content where audio-visual sync matters (tutorials)       |
| **L-cut** (video precedes audio) | Lingering emotional impact; reaction shots; montages | When viewer needs clear audio-visual pairing              |
| **Match cut**                    | Visual continuity between scenes; transformation     | Unrelated scenes with no compositional link               |
| **Whip pan**                     | TikTok/Reels; energy; scene transitions              | Professional/corporate content; tutorials                 |
| **Glitch/digital**               | Trendy social content; tech brands                   | Traditional brand content; trust-building videos          |

## Caption Styling

### The Hormozi Pattern (Proven for Social)

Specific implementation used by Alex Hormozi, widely adopted for short-form engagement:

| Element   | Specification                                                                 |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Font      | Montserrat Bold, uppercase                                                    |
| Colors    | Yellow (#f7c204) for emphasis, white for body, green (#02fb23) for highlights |
| Shadow    | Slight drop shadow + colored stroke                                           |
| Animation | Pop-in from bottom, word-by-word highlight                                    |
| Placement | Center-lower frame                                                            |

**Why it works**: Bold type + vibrant colors create immediate visual impact in silent/muted viewing. Color emphasis on key words guides attention without requiring audio.

### Caption Formatting Specs

| Element        | Specification                                        | Why                                                 |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Font family    | Sans-serif (Montserrat, Arial, Roboto)               | Outperforms serif for on-screen readability         |
| Weight         | Bold or semi-bold                                    | Legibility at small sizes and on moving backgrounds |
| Size           | 22-28pt (HD/4K); 42px+ for burnt-in accessibility    | Size has greatest impact on legibility              |
| Max words/line | ~30 characters; 2 lines maximum                      | Prevents reading fatigue                            |
| Reading speed  | 3-4 words/second; 200-250 WPM on mobile              | Matches average mobile reading speed                |
| Display timing | Min 1s, max 6s per caption (sweet spot: 2-3s)        | Below 1s = unreadable; above 6s = stale             |
| Contrast       | 4.5:1 minimum (WCAG AA); white on black = 21:1 ideal | Accessibility and readability on any background     |

### Platform Safe Zones

| Platform        | Safe Text Area               | Danger Zones                                           |
| --------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| YouTube Shorts  | Center third of screen       | Top 20% (title/channel); bottom 25% (buttons/comments) |
| Instagram Reels | Central 70-80% of 1080x1920  | Bottom 10-15% (UI buttons); top area (username)        |
| TikTok          | Similar to Reels             | Bottom 15% (description/buttons); top 10% (FYP toggle) |
| Standard 16:9   | 80% inner frame (title safe) | Outer 10% each edge (action safe margin)               |

### Burnt-In vs Sidecar Captions

| Format       | Use When                                                  | Advantage                                                           |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Burnt-in** | Social media, silent-first, outdoor/event screens         | Always visible; consistent appearance; no platform rendering issues |
| **SRT**      | YouTube, professional distribution, wide platform support | Viewer toggle; searchable; editable post-upload                     |
| **VTT**      | Web players, tutorials needing hyperlinks/styling         | Richer formatting; supports hotkeys and links                       |

### Auto-Captioning Tools

| Tool                 | Accuracy               | Best For                                        | Open Source |
| -------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| **Whisper** (OpenAI) | ~95% (5% WER)          | Multilingual, technical terms, batch processing | Yes         |
| **Descript**         | ~98%                   | Podcasts, interviews, transcript-based editing  | No          |
| **Rev**              | ~99% (hybrid AI+human) | Legal, medical, academic                        | No          |
| **CapCut**           | Lower                  | TikTok/Reels, fast mobile turnaround            | No          |

> **Tool pricing and accuracy change frequently.** Whisper is the stable open-source choice. For commercial tools, check current pricing and test accuracy with your specific content before committing.

## Royalty-Free Music Sources

| Source                             | License Type                     | Best For                                          |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Epidemic Sound**                 | All-rights-included subscription | Multi-platform commercial use; avoids Content ID  |
| **Artlist**                        | Lifetime rights per track        | Comprehensive production (music + SFX + footage)  |
| **Uppbeat**                        | Freemium                         | Budget-conscious creators; no demonetization risk |
| **YouTube Audio Library**          | Free, YouTube-only license       | YouTube-only videos                               |
| **Pixabay / Pexels**               | Free, community-driven           | Simple projects, limited selection                |
| **AI generation (AIVA, Soundraw)** | Varies by tier                   | Original compositions; no copyright matches       |

> **Licensing changes.** Verify commercial-use terms on each platform's current licensing page. Key pitfall: social platform sounds (TikTok, Instagram built-in) are typically personal-use only — not licensed for commercial ads.
